# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Language learners who use spoken video or audio as comprehensible input. They may work on desktop or mobile and need to turn a source lesson into a visual, replayable study artifact without managing a complex media-production workflow.

## Product Purpose

Visualang turns a YouTube source or uploaded audio file into a transcript-driven illustrated video. It extracts concrete visual moments, generates storybook imagery, provides a synchronized preview, and exports the finished video with its transcript and images. Success means the user can follow long-running processing with confidence, review the result, and leave with a useful downloadable learning artifact.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, humane, focused.

The experience should also feel intelligent, tactile, editorial, and calm under load. Its language should be direct and reassuring without presenting the system as magical.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI startup interfaces built around purple gradients, cosmic glows, sparkles, or “magic” language
- Chatbot, prompt-playground, agent-dashboard, and token-pill interaction models
- Floating glass cards, empty center-column SaaS heroes, and decorative metrics that obscure the workflow
- Generic content-generation framing that makes language learning or the exported video feel secondary
- Noisy progress messaging, low-contrast controls, or motion that competes with the user’s task

## Design Principles

1. **Make the learning artifact the outcome.** Emphasize what the user is making and the downloadable video rather than the underlying AI pipeline.
2. **Keep the workflow unmistakable.** Source input, processing, preview, export, and download should form one legible sequence with clear state changes.
3. **Earn trust during waiting.** Long-running steps need concise progress, useful status, and recoverable failures without visual noise.
4. **Show language learning through the experience.** Copy, imagery, and controls should feel specific to turning spoken material into comprehensible visual study content.
5. **Use craft in service of focus.** Cinematic and tactile details should strengthen hierarchy and comprehension, never distract from the current task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard operation, visible high-contrast focus states, readable text contrast, semantic headings, status announcements, and layouts that do not rely on color alone. State changes should move focus deliberately, file upload must remain clearly operable, and every non-essential animation needs a reduced-motion alternative. Desktop and mobile experiences are both required.
